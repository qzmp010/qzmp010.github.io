What Is Computer Vision?

Computer Vision is a branch of Artificial Intelligence (AI) that enables machines to interpret and understand visual information from the world — just like humans do with their eyes and brain. Using cameras and advanced algorithms, a computer vision system can detect, recognize, and track objects such as people, vehicles, road signs, and traffic lights.

In simple terms, computer vision allows a machine to see, analyze, and act based on what it observes.

What Are Autonomous Vehicles?

An autonomous vehicle (often called a self-driving car) is a car that can sense its environment and navigate without human input. These vehicles rely on a combination of sensors, cameras, radar, and AI software to make driving decisions.

There are five levels of driving automation, defined by the Society of Automotive Engineers (SAE):

  1. Level 0 - No automation (human fully in control).
  2. Level 1 - Driver assistance (e.g., cruise control).
  3. Level 2 - Partial automation (lane centering + speed control).
  4. Level 3 - Conditional automation (car drives itself but may need human intervention).
  5. Level 4-5 - High and full automation (no human driver required).

Most current cars, such as Tesla’s Autopilot, are around Level 2 or Level 3.
