Challenges and Future of Computer Vision in AVs

Home How It Works Applications Challenges & Future


Current Challenges

  • Adverse Weather & Lighting: rain, fog, snow, glare.
  • Long-tail Events: rare, unpredictable edge cases.
  • Occlusion: objects hidden behind others.
  • Domain Shift: new cities/countries look different.
  • Latency & Compute: real-time inference on vehicle hardware.
  • Safety & Verification: proving performance at scale.
  • Privacy & Security: safeguarding data & resisting attacks.

What Helps Mitigate These?

Challenge Technique Impact
Weather/Lighting Sensor diversity; polarization; image enhancement Improves robustness when vision degrades
Long-tail Events Simulation, synthetic data, continual learning Exposes models to rare scenarios safely
Occlusion Tracking + prediction; multi-view fusion Maintains awareness of partially hidden objects
Domain Shift Domain adaptation; map/context priors Transfers performance to new geographies
Latency/Compute Model optimization, quantization, acceleration Meets real-time constraints on embedded hardware
Safety Assurance Scenario coverage, formal methods, staged rollout Builds evidence for reliability

Future Trends

  • End-to-End Learning: train perception-to-plan pipelines with safety guards.
  • V2X Connectivity: cars communicate with cars/infrastructure to “see” beyond line of sight.
  • Edge + Cloud: smart offloading and fleet learning improve models continuously.
  • Cheaper Sensors: solid-state LiDAR & high-res radar reduce costs.
  • Regulatory Frameworks: clearer testing/operations rules expand deployment zones.
